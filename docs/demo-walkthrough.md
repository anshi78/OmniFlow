# 🎭 OmniFlow AI — Hackathon Demo Walkthrough

This document outlines a step-by-step walkthrough script you can use to pitch **OmniFlow AI** to judges.

---

## 🏁 Demo Script

### Step 1: The Command Center (Dashboard)
1. Navigate to the landing page at `http://localhost:3000`.
2. **Point out the aesthetics**: Modern dark theme, glassmorphic stats cards, active shipment counts, and the real-time AI Agent Activity Feed on the right.
3. Show the **7-Day Demand Forecast Chart**: Point out the shaded confidence intervals generated by our **XGBoost** model.

### Step 2: Live Stock Sheets (Inventory)
1. Click **Inventory** in the sidebar.
2. Search for any product (e.g. `Wireless Headphones`).
3. Point out the color-coded **Status** column (Green = Normal, Amber = Low, Red = Critical) and the dynamically calculated **Days of Supply**.

### Step 3: Triggering a Supply Disruption (Simulation)
1. Click **Simulation Center** in the sidebar.
2. Select **Demand Spike Simulation** (which simulates a 3-5x demand increase).
3. Click **Run Simulation**.
4. **Watch the live agent coordination**:
   - The **Demand Forecast Agent** flags the demand surge.
   - The **Store Agent** generates emergency restock requests.
   - The **Warehouse Agent** intercepts the requests and allocates shipping lanes.
   - The **Orchestrator** compiles the action plan.
5. Show the **Impact Visualization**: Highlight the calculated Lost Revenue risk and the dynamic replenishment actions.

### Step 4: Inside the Brain (Agent Monitor)
1. Click **Agent Monitor** in the sidebar.
2. Show the **Agent Communication Graph**: Visualizes how individual agents talk to the Orchestrator.
3. Scroll through the **Event Timeline**: Displays the exact reasoning, confidence score, and timestamp of every single automated supply chain decision.

### Step 5: Explainable AI (AI Insights)
1. Click **AI Insights** in the sidebar.
2. Show the **AI Recommendations**: Explain how every auto-restock recommendation features confidence ratings and an expandable "Why did AI decide this?" panel explaining the specific factors (e.g., proximity, seasonality, low stock) that influenced the agent's decision.
